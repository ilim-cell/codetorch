# Quick Start - PolyOS 

Welcome to your new PolyOS 7 operating system! This guide will help you get started with the basics of using PolyOS 7, including how to navigate the interface, use built-in apps, and customize your settings.

## Initial setup
Start the PolyOS 7 operating system by booting it up using the green flag button on your computer. A BIOS screen may appear. 
> [!TIP]
> If there is a popup asking you to "Press ENTER for advanced recovery options", press [ESC] to skip it.

PolyOS 7 will then load the main interface for the setup Out of Box Experience.

### Have you used PolyOS 7 before?
If you have used PolyOS 7 before, you can select the "Load a save code" option to load your previous settings and preferences. If you are new to PolyOS 7, select "Install PolyOS 7" to begin with the default settings.

#### Installing PolyOS 7
If you are installing PolyOS 7 for the first time, follow the on-screen instructions to agree to the license agreement, set up your user account password, and configure your appearance preferences. Once the installation is complete, you will be taken to the main interface of PolyOS 7.

#### Continuing where you've left off
Enter a save code in the "Load a save code" option to continue where you left off. This will load your previous settings and preferences, allowing you to pick up right where you left off.

## Lock Screen
The Lock Screen of PolyOS 7 is designed to provide a secure and convenient way to access your operating system. When you start up PolyOS 7, you will be presented with the Lock Screen, which displays the current time and date, information about system usage and news, as well as a password prompt for your user account.
Click anywhere on the Lock Screen to bring up the password prompt. Enter your user account password to unlock the operating system and access the main interface. If you have forgotten your password, you can use the "Forgot Password" option to reset it.

> [!WARNING]
> Due to the nature of the disk encryption, the Forgot Password option will reset your entire desktop and may result in the loss of your previous settings and preferences. Use this option with caution.

## Navigating the interface
The main interface of PolyOS 7 is designed to be simple and intuitive. You can use the mouse or touchpad to navigate through the interface, and the keyboard for shortcuts and commands.
The core interface of PolyOS 7 consists of the Dock at the bottom of the screen, which contains your pinned apps, the Home menu, and the launcher for accessing all installed apps.
The Home menu contains time and date information, quick shortcuts to Vara Assistant and command execution terminal, volume and brightness controls, power options, and a save button for dumping your current session to disk. It is recommended to use this feature to preserve your work. It also contains 10 of your most used apps.
The launcher allows you to access all installed apps, including built-in apps and any custom apps you have installed. You can also search for apps using the search bar at the top of the launcher.

## Using built-in apps
PolyOS 7 comes with a variety of built-in apps that you can use to perform various tasks. These currently include Settings, Files, PolyMarket, Wallpapers, SysManager, ExperimentalEditor, Camera, News, Community App Launcher, Terminal, XPackage Installer, and XappWindow 3.0 demo. You can access these apps from the launcher or by searching for them in the search bar.

When an app is open, you can interact with it using the mouse or touchpad, and use keyboard shortcuts for various functions. You can also resize and move app windows around the screen to customize your workspace. Use the two options in the top right corner to maximise or close a window.

## Customizing your settings
Open the Settings app from the Launcher or the Home menu to customize your settings and preferences. You can change your appearance, set up user accounts, configure developer options, and more. Explore the various settings to personalize your PolyOS 7 experience. Settings apply instantly when you click them rather than having to save them.

## Shutting down PolyOS 7
To shut down PolyOS 7, click on the power button in the Home menu and select "Shut Down". This will safely close all apps and ask you whether you want to save your data in the format of a savecode before powering off the operating system. You can also use the "Lock" option to safely lock your desktop of PolyOS 7 if needed.
